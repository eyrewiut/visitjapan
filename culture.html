History

During the Edo period, Japan exercised a strict isolation policy which removed it from all outside relationships and influence, this meant that a distinctly Japanese culture was able to form like few other cultures in the world.

After the end of the Edo period, Japan completely reversed this policy and let outside influence flood in creating a fascinating merge of modern and ancient tradition, architecture and language. In addition, it welcomed relationships with other countries which has developed the modern Japan we know today.
