Kurashiki canal area - Okayama

くらしき - おかやま

Kurishiki is a beautiful old style city in the okayama prefecture and has a pretty canal flowing through, and quite a lot for tourists to do. The canal was built in the edo period (1603-1867) to transport rice and grain as kurashiki's main purpose was as a trade city

Gujo hachiman - Gifu

ぐじょはちまん - ぎじゅ

Gujo hachiman is often referred to as "The Water City" as it is a rustic town built right around a large river that comes directly from the mountains above, this clean water is a central part of life in gujo hachiman and there are streams and wells alongside every street, it is common to see people fishing for ayu in the river, these are then sold on sticks in the city. Even the gutters of The Water City are clean enough to be enhabited by hundreds of large koi fish which can be fed for a small fee.

Ichijodani Asakura Clan ruins - Fukui

あさくら - ふくい

During the Muromachi period, this castle town was the home of the powerful Asakura clan, during the time, the town was the center of the region's cultureal, military and commercial industries and had over 10,000 residents. However, another powerful clan daimyo named Oda Nobunaga burnt down the entire town in his fight to control all of japan. A small section of the town where upper class citizens such as samurai would have lived has been accurately restored and the site has been marked as a historic site. Visitors here can dress up like members of the samurai caste of the time!
